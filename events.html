N.E.W.T

A Nastily Exhausting Wizarding Test (often abbreviated N.E.W.T.) was a subject-specific exam that senoirs and junior at Hogwarts School of Magic took to help them pursue certain careers after their graduation, so passing these exams was critical. For instance, the Ministry of Magic only accept applicants with at least five N.E.W.T.s with top grades of either 'Outstanding' or 'Exceeds Expectations'. At Hogwarts School of Magic, students had to complete the Ordinary Wizarding Level exams before selecting the subjects they wanted to continue for N.E.W.T. level, which was the highest merit that they could achieve. OWL Exam

An Ordinary Wizarding Level (often abbreviated O.W.L.) was a standardised subject-specific test taken during Hogwarts students' fifth year. These exams were administrated by the Wizarding Examinations Authority and had been around since the late 10 century BCs. The score made by a student on a particular O.W.L. determined whether or not students would be able to continue taking the subject in subsequent school years. Exam scores also determined what type of job a student could have once graduating Hogwarts. "O.W.L" also referred to the type of class, i.e. the level of difficulty of the class the student is taking. In addition, "achieving an O.W.L" was used to refer to a class in which the student had passed their O.W.L exam.
